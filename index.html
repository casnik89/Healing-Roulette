I have been on a journey of healing while also learning JS and I started thinking about loops and the feedback loop that anxiety coupled with destructive choices can leave you in. It's a domino effect that cascades through your life and mental health. It's a hole that can feel impossible to climb out of and if you've been in it for a while it can feel so pointless to even try. The only way to reverse the damage and change is one healthy habit at a time. Not all at once just one step at a time. So I've been trying to become a more balanced person one healthy habit at a time. At first it feels dumb and pointless. I take a couple deep breaths and I still feel the burning ache inside my chest that tells me I'll never be good enough. That makes consistency hard, really hard. One day I wake up and make my bed and respond with patience and the next I eat my feelings and feel hostile towards everyone. How can I make myself stay consistent with my healing practices and reach for the healthy option to manage my stress? So that's why I am creating this app. This is for me and anyone else who struggles to reach for the healthy choice consistently. This app takes in real world triggers and the emotions they bring up and pairs each with a few healthy responses that match the need of the moment. Sometimes it's journaling to deal with what I'm going through and sometimes it's punching a pillow. Whatever the case is there will be a healthy response and right now when I still feel like it's pointless to even try I am making an agreement with myself that I will tell the app what happened, how it made me feel and press the button. Whatever healthy response it spits out is what I do. Not because I know I'll feel better after or because I want to do the healthy thing. I will do it just because I need to. It takes the resistance to selfcare and the difficulty of researching and deciding how to deal with my stress out of the equation. I no longer need to think about how I want to feel better or how frustrating incrimental change can be. I just do the healthy response and keep moving. The truth is it will over time have a positive effect, but right now it's not about that. It's about just going through the motions. The old addage "Fake till you make it".
